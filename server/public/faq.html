FAQ

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event in which a large number of people meet together for collaborative programming. This hackathon is created to get more students involved in computer science and sharing ideas with other people.

How much does it cost to attend the hackathon?

Nothing! It’s true, this hackathon is made possible by the help of dozens of generous sponsors and mentors that all believe that education should be free for everyone.

Where do I signup? Can I bring a friend?

Please signup using the website’s registration page. Friends are only allowed if they are apart of the El Dorado School District. Due to safety concerns we will not be allowed to accept anyone outside of the district, but don’t worry, you’re in safe hands.

How long does the event last?

The event will be 24 hours long! Don’t worry if you get sleepy, 24 hours is a long time, there will be rooms specifically for sleeping if you need a rest. Feel free to bring blankets, pillows and whatever you like to make yourself comfortable or even build your own fort.

Do I have to bring my own meals?

No, all meals are being provided by the hackathon preparation team along with plenty of snacks for everyone. There will also be vegetarian meal options, please include this information when you signup on the registration page of the website.

Where is the event located?

Please see the Location page of the website for more information, it includes pictures of the site as well as parking information.

What can I do if I’m not that good at programming?

Over the course of the hackathon, there will be presentations and seminars designed to teach new students programming techniques, practices, and business tools. If you are more advanced, there are other courses being taught as well.
